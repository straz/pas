Cyber Operations

an online course by Paul A. Strassmann

Overview

In the last two years Paul A. Strassmann delivered five courses on Cyber Operations, validating the effectiveness of interactive on-line teaching about topics related to the safeguarding of national security systems. With completion of testing, involving over a hundred students, this course is now available for sponsorship by organizations interested in executive development of personnel in dealing with cyber matters.

The Cyber Operations courses were offered to graduate students in the George Mason University School of Engineering and in the AFCEA (Armed Forces Communications and Electronics Association) Professional Development Center. The courses were listed in the catalogues of both organizations.

Scope of the Course

The lessons provide students with a technical overview of the current cyber issues as they relate to policy-level and managerial issues. The course content deals with topics that are of primary interest to the Department of Defense personnel, the Department of Homeland Security, National Intelligence organizations and its contractors who will be carrying much of the responsibility for actual implementation of cyber-related systems.

The subject matter covered is diverse. Each weekly session is two and a half hours. Curriculum can be offered at different levels of security. The sessions:

  1. Introductions to the Course on Cyber Operations
  2. Information Technology for Cyber Operations
  3. Attacks on Cyber Security
  4. Internet Networks
  5. Networks and Cyber Operations
  6. Security of Cyber Operations
  7. Cyber Operations on Data Center Clouds
  8. Virtualization and Legacy Systems
  9. Virtual Desktop Infrastructure in Cyber Operations
  10. Natural Language Processing
  11. Data and Storage Management
  12. Open Source Software
  13. Collaboration in Cyber Operations

There are more than 75 slides shown in every class and are provided a week ahead of each class so that students can be prepared to ask questions. Students are able to annotate the presentation material with a real-time on-line “chat” commentary.

Course Benefits

Students will acquire an understanding of how the latest information technologies apply to cyber operations. Emphasis will be placed on the most recent technological developments, such as cloud computing, defensive countermeasures, zero-day vulnerabilities, cyber threats and the available technological means to systems security. The presentations will not cover detailed technical topics that can be found in existing textbooks. Concentration will be on the most recent developments, including reviews of cyber attacks as they occur.

This course can be seen as a career enhancement for personnel seeking added skills to qualify for managerial positions that are now opening in support of cyber operations.

Course Logistics

Courses will run weekly in evening from 7:30PM to 9:30PM (Eastern Time) for thirteen consecutive sessions using either Blackboard or CISCO Webex groupware.

Students will receive a personal invitation with secure links for each class.

Course Requirements

Students must have at least four years of prior experience working with information technologies. Students will have to respond, in writing, to two quizzes about the topics covered in each session. A student who cannot attend will receive a password for a recording.

Every student’s desktop must come equipped with a camera. During class discussion the student’s picture will appear on the screen.

Students will see on their desktops the instructor’s presentation outline (usually slides), white boards and space for write-in comments. When a student has a question they can click on their message to prompt the instructor for a response.

Depending how a sponsor organizes classes it is possible for written comments to be graded. Graduate level grades, for academic credit from an accredited institution, are also available.

About the Instructor

Paul A. Strassmann is Distinguished Professor at George Mason University. He served as Director of Defense Information in the Office of the Secretary of Defense. He was Acting CIO of NASA, and VP and CIO of Xerox Corporation.
